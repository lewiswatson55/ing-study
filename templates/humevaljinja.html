Evaluating Text Quality

Information

Experiment: Generated Text Quality 2024-06

Lead Researcher: Dave Howcroft (D.Howcroft@napier.ac.uk)

Study purpose

We are developing text generation systems for English. In order to improve these systems, we need to ask humans to rate the quality of the generated texts. The collected data will be used:

  • to inform the design of text generation systems;
  • to provide data for researchers interested in evaluating generated text; and
  • to train artificial intelligence (AI) systems for text generation.

Requirements

  • Everyone participating in the study must be at least 18 years old.
  • Participants must be speakers of English as a first language and living in one of the following countries: Ireland, the UK, New Zealand, the USA, Australia, or Canada.

What does participating in the study entail?

There are two main phases to this study: (1) a set of instructions and example ratings followed by (2) rating 30 texts for fluency and documenting any errors.

After completing the study, you will be paid £6 for your participation, which should take about half an hour.

Data collection and sharing

We will collect:

  • your consent through this form;
  • the ratings and comments you provide; and
  • normal server meta-data to ensure that our survey software is working as intended (including, for example, your IP address).

All the data will be stored anonymously and published anonymously as well. Demographic information about language familiarity will only be stored and published in aggregate. We will ensure that it is not possible to associate this data with you as an individual.

Rights

  • Your participation is completely voluntary, and you have the right to decline to participate and withdraw from the research once participation has begun, without any negative consequences, and without providing any explanation.
  • You will not be asked to provide sensitive data, or special category data. GDPR sensitive data is any data that reveals information such as racial or ethnic origin, political or religious beliefs, or gender or sexual orientation.
  • You are entitled to compensation by taking part in this study, based on the rate mentioned above.
  • As the data is fully anonymous, we may not be able to delete or alter your responses.

What does consent mean?

By consenting, you indicate that you are voluntarily taking part in this study, and you allow for your data to be processed. This means that:

  • You agree that your answers may be used to publish research articles on this topic.
  • The data will be stored on the computers of the research team, with both local (hard drive) and online (protected cloud drive) backups.
  • The anonymised data will be made public upon completion of this study.

Examples and Instructions

Before you start the task, let's go through an example to help you understand the input format and the type of evaluations you will be doing.

On every page you will see a section labeled "Input" which lists some facts taht are supposed to be included in the text. "Entity" entries will be different people, places, and things (nouns) while the "Relation" entries will describe the relationship between the two entities. Here's the general structure.

Input:

Entity1

Relation

Entity2

For example, the input could be:

Paris

is the capital of

France

After the input section, you will see an "Output" section, which shows the text generated by one of our systems for that input.

Output:

Paris is the capital of France, home of la Tour Eiffel.

Your task in this survey is to rate these Output texts for their fluency and to document any errors in how the Output expresses the Input. For example, the text above is generally fluent, so it would be appropriate to give it a score of "Fluent" on the scale. Please do that for this example.

Fluency Rating:

After rating the fluency, you will need to identify what (if any) parts of the input are missing in the output. The input will be presented for you as a table, and you must select next to each part of the input whether it is Present, Missing, or Incorrect. For this example, all 3 parts are present, so please mark them accordingly.

Part Status:

Identify if any part is missing or incorrect in the output. For example:

Part Status
Paris
is the capital of
France

Finally, you will need to let us know if any information which was not present in the Input was included in the Output. In this example, the answer is "yes" because the fact that Paris is home to the Eiffel tower (la Tour Eiffel) is not a part of the input. Please check yes and state what was added.
